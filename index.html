Reviewer #4: Why not distinguish type #0 and type #2 CVD cells by check the convexity on projected 2D shape? & Isn't it just enough to partition the projected 2D polygon?
R: A very good question. Some explanation can be found in Q3. In fact, if a RVD cell is nearly planar, it is no problem to project it onto a plane. But the kidney shaped cells often occur near the edge of a thin shape. I understand what you mean: why not consider the re-partitioning step on the 2D parameterized domain (in fact, projection also leads to a simple parameterization result)? Our response is: a non-isometric parameterization cannot work (to guarantee that RVD is close to GVD), but an as-isometric-as-possible parameterization result is non-trivial to obtain and computationally time-consuming, especially when the local shape is highly curved.
